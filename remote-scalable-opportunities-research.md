**What you're selling:**
- AI-powered platform to create interactive calculators, assessments, and quizzes
- No-code builder with AI generation
- Embeddable widgets for websites
- Lead capture + CRM integrations (Zapier, HubSpot, Mailchimp)
- Analytics dashboard

- **Financial advisors:** "Retirement readiness quiz", "Investment risk assessment"

**Differentiation:**
- AI generates the entire assessment from a simple prompt
- Beautiful templates (not ugly calculator widgets)
- Built-in lead nurturing sequences
- Conversion optimization built-in (A/B testing, personalization)

## 💡 Solo-Founder Success Patterns (Lessons from the Winners)

### What Successful Indie Hackers Did Right

**1. Started with ONE clear problem**
- Pieter Levels: Nomad List solved "where should digital nomads go?"
- Tony Dinh: Typing Mind solved "better UI for ChatGPT"
- Marc Lou: ShipFast solved "ship fast without boilerplate"

**2. Shipped fast, iterated faster**
- MVPs in 2-4 weeks
- Launched publicly (Product Hunt, Twitter)
- Got feedback, improved daily
- "Done is better than perfect"

**3. Built in public**
- Shared revenue numbers openly
- Tweeted progress daily
- Grew following = free marketing
- Helped others = karma paid back

**4. Product-led growth, not sales-led**
- Free trials or freemium
- Users could try before buying
- Viral loops (users invite users)
- No sales calls needed

**5. Focused on distribution from day one**
- SEO from launch (content, keywords)
- Community presence (Reddit, Twitter, forums)
- Partnerships (integrations, affiliates)
- Paid ads only after product-market fit

**6. Kept it simple**
- One product, one core feature
- Said no to most feature requests
- Automated everything possible
- Outsourced only when necessary

**7. Charged from day one**
- No "build for 6 months then figure out pricing"
- Validated willingness to pay immediately
- Increased prices over time
- Annual plans for cash flow

### Common Mistakes They Avoided

❌ **Building for too long without customers**
- Shipped MVP in weeks, not months
- Got paying customers before perfecting product

❌ **Trying to serve everyone**
- Picked a niche and owned it
- Said no to customers outside ICP

❌ **Premature scaling (hiring too early)**
- Stayed solo as long as possible
- Only hired when drowning in support

❌ **Complex sales processes**
- Self-serve or bust
- If it needed demos/calls, they pivoted

❌ **Ignoring marketing**
- Built distribution alongside product
- Content, SEO, community from day one
